Project Overview

Building a functional, interactive, and smart electronic device powered by Raspberry Pi Pico

The Challenge

This project began with a challenge from our professor: design and build a functional, interactive, portable, and smart electronic device powered by the Raspberry Pi Pico. The goal was to create a device that not only works reliably, but also demonstrates our understanding of Python programming, electronic components, AutoCAD design, and the full Engineering Design Process.

Design Specifications

  • Laser-cut enclosure designed in AutoCAD with proper openings and assembly
  • Raspberry Pi Pico integration with minimum 4 electronic components
  • User interface with input methods and feedback systems
  • Complete digital design notebook documentation

Design Iterations

Six major iterations that transformed our concept into a functional prototype

Iteration 1

Initial Breadboard Assembly

Circuit assembly using a full-size breadboard

This iteration focused on assembling and testing the circuit components directly on a full-size breadboard to verify basic functionality.

Iteration 2

Early Cardboard Enclosure

Rough enclosure prototype using preliminary measurements

Key Findings

  • Full-size breadboard was too bulky
  • Enclosure size would need to increase or be redesigned

Conclusion

This iteration showed that a different internal approach was required to maintain portability.

Iteration 3

Mini Breadboard Conversion

Reduced footprint and improved enclosure feasibility

Improvements

  • Significantly reduced internal spacing issues
  • Allowed for a smaller enclosure design

New Challenge

Inconsistent wire lengths caused clutter and made internal organization difficult.

Iteration 4

Revised Enclosure Height

Improved clearance and wiring management

The enclosure was made slightly taller to improve internal clearance, making wiring cleaner and reducing interference between components.

Iteration 5

Sliding Internal Frame

First wooden prototype with modular internal structure

Design Advancements

  • Hidden wiring for a cleaner appearance
  • Space added for LCD screen and candy dispenser

Challenges

Layered structure complicated wire routing and caused components to disconnect, requiring soldered connections and a redesigned candy dispenser mechanism.

Iteration 6

Final Integrated Design

Stable, functional, and demonstration-ready system

All solutions were combined into the final design, resulting in a reliable device with secure wiring, clean internal organization, and fully functional gameplay.

What Worked Well

  • Measuring before cutting
  • Flat ribbon cables for neater organization
  • Flowcharting before coding

What Didn't Work

  • Ignoring debouncing early on
  • Attempting to fit everything on one plane
  • Jumping into wiring before planning internal layout

Final System Overview

The final design features a Raspberry Pi Pico microcontroller, sixteen LEDs (twelve active), an LCD display with a front viewing window, two arcade buttons, and a servo-controlled candy dispenser, all housed within a custom laser-cut wooden enclosure. The internal structure is carefully organized to support each component securely, and the device runs a fully functional reaction-based game. The final product is portable, durable, and ready for live demonstration.
